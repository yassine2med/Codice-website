## Design Vision & North Star

### Aesthetic Direction
**"Palantir meets Vercel, built for DC government."**

Not startup-flashy. Not enterprise-boring. Something in between:
- Authoritative enough for a procurement officer
- Sophisticated enough for a CTO
- Confident enough that your boss can't say no

### Reference Sites (study these for aesthetic direction)
| Site | What to steal |
|---|---|
| **palantir.com** | Dark, data-forward, show-don't-tell philosophy, HUD-inspired UI |
| **vercel.com** | Clean grid, micro-animations, minimal but premium |
| **anduril.com** | Dark industrial, bold typography, product-first |
| **linear.app** | Micro-animation quality, smooth transitions, product polish |
| **stripe.com** | Trust signals, social proof structure, gradient usage |

### The "Show Don't Tell" Rule
Inspired by the Palantir redesign that produced 1000% more leads:
> "Rather than defining the company, define the Codice-shaped problems they solve."

Every section should answer: **"What does this make possible for government?"** — not just list a feature.

### Tone & Voice
- **Confident, not arrogant**
- **Proven, not boastful** (let the stats and clients speak)
- **Precise, not corporate** (no "synergize", no "leverage", no "paradigm")
- **DC-native** (they know this city and its agencies — show it)

### What Makes This Site Impossible to Copy
1. The 100% client retention badge — front and center
2. Real client logos (DC agencies people recognize)
3. 8 named proprietary products with their own identities
4. 16 years of case studies with specific outcomes
5. The DC Small Business of the Year award — visible immediately

## 2025-2026 Gov Tech Web Design Trends (Research-backed)

- **Security-centric design:** Encryption/compliance messaging builds trust
- **Dark mode + data aesthetics:** Neon-on-navy color schemes signal serious tech
- **AI-first positioning:** Companies that lead with AI products (like FortiMind.ai) are winning bids
- **Interactive product demos:** Agencies want to see before they buy
- **Mobile-first:** Government procurement now happens on tablets and phones
- **Conversational navigation:** Users explore by topic, not by menu
- **Bold stat storytelling:** Numbers with context beat paragraphs of copy
